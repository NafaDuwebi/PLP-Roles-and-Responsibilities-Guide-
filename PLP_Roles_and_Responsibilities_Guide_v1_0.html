Who does what in our QMS?

This guide explains the roles and responsibilities across PL Projects in plain language. Whether you are new to the team or just want a clear reminder, this page tells you who owns each process, what each person is responsible for, and how every role fits into our ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System.

New to the QMS? Start here. This page is designed to be easy to follow even if you have never worked with a Quality Management System before. Use the tabs at the top to explore different areas.

What is the QMS and why does it matter?

Our Quality Management System (QMS) is the set of processes, procedures, and responsibilities that ensure PL Projects consistently delivers high-quality training and consultancy to our clients. It also helps us maintain our ISO 9001:2015 accreditation, certified by SGS.

📋

Processes & procedures

Every key activity at PL Projects is documented as a process — from receiving a training enquiry all the way to reviewing how a course went. These are stored in our Process Architecture on SharePoint.

👤

Process owners

Each process has a named owner — someone responsible for making sure the process is followed and kept up to date. If you are a process owner, your name appears in this guide.

🔍

Internal audits

Each year, every process is reviewed through an internal audit. This checks that we are following our own procedures and identifies anything we can improve. Everyone may be asked to take part.

🏅

ISO 9001 certification

Our ISO 9001:2015 accreditation is assessed annually by an external body called SGS. They check our processes are effective and our records are in order. Our current certification runs to August 2027.

📈

Continual improvement

The QMS is not just about passing audits. It helps us spot problems, record improvements, and make PL Projects a better organisation. Anyone can raise an improvement idea.

📁

Document control

Every document in the QMS has a version number and an issue date. Only the latest approved version should be used. Old documents must be removed from active use to avoid confusion.

Our two business streams

All 25 processes in the QMS are grouped into two streams. Every role at PL Projects sits within one or both of these streams.

RACI Matrix

Responsibility assignment

The RACI matrix shows, for every QMS process, who is Responsible for doing the work, who is Accountable for the outcome, who should be Consulted, and who needs to be kept Informed. It is a simple way of making sure nothing falls through the cracks.

What do R, A, C, I mean?
R Responsible — the person who actually does the work
A Accountable — the person who is ultimately answerable for the outcome (usually the process owner)
C Consulted — someone whose input is needed before decisions are made
I Informed — someone who needs to be kept updated on progress or outcomes
Simple rule: Every process must have exactly one person marked A. There can be multiple R or I entries, but only one person is ultimately accountable.

Frequently Asked Questions

For all staff

Common questions about roles, responsibilities, and the QMS — answered in plain language. Click any question to expand the answer.

A process owner is the person responsible for a specific QMS process. It means you are accountable for making sure the process is being followed correctly in day-to-day work, that the procedure document stays accurate and up to date, and that any nonconformities or improvements raised against your process are addressed on time.


In practice, your main responsibilities are: (1) co-operating with the internal auditor when your process is being reviewed each year, (2) reviewing your procedure documents periodically and flagging if anything needs updating, and (3) raising an improvement on the Improvement Log if you spot a problem or a better way of working.


You do not need to be a QMS expert. The Quality Manager (Nafa) is available to support you at every step.

An internal audit is a structured review of how well a QMS process is being followed. Every year, each of our 25 processes goes through an internal audit. The audit is conducted by a trained auditor and the process owner, and it checks whether the documented procedure matches what actually happens in practice.


If you are a process owner, you will be directly involved. The auditor will ask you questions about how the process works and may ask to see evidence (emails, forms, records) that the steps have been followed. If you are a team member who carries out work in a process, you may also be asked questions — this is normal and nothing to be concerned about.


The output of an audit is a report with a conformity score and any nonconformities or improvement ideas noted. Nonconformities are not personal criticisms — they are system findings that the process owner then works to resolve.

A nonconformity (sometimes called an NC) is a finding where the evidence shows that a process step is not being followed as documented, or a required record does not exist. It is not a disciplinary issue — it is a quality system finding that needs to be investigated and resolved.


If a nonconformity is raised against your process: (1) it will be logged on the Active Improvement Log by the Quality Manager, (2) you will be given a target date to resolve it, (3) you need to identify why it happened (root cause), implement a fix, and provide evidence that the fix has worked. The Quality Manager will then verify and close the item.


Unresolved nonconformities can become findings at the external SGS audit, so it is important to address them promptly.

Our internal audits are carried out by our own trained staff each year across all 25 processes. They are planned and managed by Nafa (Quality Manager) and are designed to spot any gaps before an external body visits.


The SGS audit (our external audit) is conducted by an independent body called SGS, who assess whether PL Projects meets the requirements of ISO 9001:2015. This happens approximately once a year as a surveillance visit. SGS can raise their own nonconformities and observations — these carry more weight and must be resolved within the agreed timescale.


Think of the internal audit as a rehearsal that prepares us for the SGS visit. Our current SGS certification is valid until August 2027.

All current QMS documents are stored on our SharePoint site in the QMS section — accessible from the left-hand menu. The Process Architecture page shows all process flowcharts and procedures in their latest approved versions.


Always use the document from the SharePoint QMS area. Do not use copies saved on your personal desktop or email attachments — these may be out of date. Every QMS document has a version number and issue date on it. If you are unsure which version is current, contact Nafa.


If you notice a document that appears outdated, incorrect, or missing, please raise it with Nafa so it can be reviewed and updated through the formal document control process.

Anyone at PL Projects can raise an improvement. If you spot something that could be done better, or notice a problem with a process, you can either: (1) speak directly to the process owner or the Quality Manager (Nafa), or (2) use the improvement log process — Nafa can log it on your behalf if needed.


Every improvement idea goes on the Active Improvement Log and is given a priority rating, an owner, and a target date. It is then tracked until it is implemented and closed. You will be kept informed of progress if you raised the item.


Do not wait for an audit to flag a problem. Continual improvement is everyone's responsibility — and raising things early is always better than waiting for an auditor to find them.

ISO 9001:2015 requires that the organisation has documented processes, follows them, and can demonstrate this through records. As a staff member, your main obligations under ISO 9001 are:


1. Follow the documented procedure for any process you are involved in. If the procedure does not match how you actually work, tell the process owner — do not just work around it.


2. Keep records as required by your process. For example, if a process asks you to complete a form or log something in a system, make sure this is done consistently.


3. Be aware of the Quality Policy. PL Projects' Quality Policy sets out our commitment to customers. All staff are expected to understand it — it is published on SharePoint.


4. Engage with audits. If you are asked to take part in an internal or external audit, co-operate fully and answer questions honestly. Auditors are not trying to catch you out — they are checking the system, not the person.

For any question about the QMS, process documents, audits, or the Improvement Log, the first point of contact is Nafa Duwebi (Quality Manager). Nafa manages the day-to-day operation of the QMS and can answer most questions or direct you to the right person.


For questions about a specific process — for example, how a training enquiry should be handled — speak to the process owner for that process. The process ownership table on the Process Ownership tab shows who owns each process.


For strategic decisions about the QMS or significant proposed changes, Andy Pochin (Director) provides final sign-off.
